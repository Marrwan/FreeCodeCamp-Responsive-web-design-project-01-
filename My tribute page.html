Ibn Sina

Father of modern medicine

Ibn sina(ابن سينا), also known as Abu Ali Sina and often known in the west as Avicenna was a persian polymath who is regarded as one of the most significant physicians, astronomers, thinkers and writers of Islamic Golden Age and father of early modern medicine.They regarded him as the principal respresentative of philosophy in Islam. He is also called "the most influential philosopher of the pre-modern era". He was a peripatethic phulosopher influenced by Aristotelian philosophy. Of the 450 works he is believed to have written, around 240 have survived, including 150 on philosophy and 40 on medicine.

Here is a timeline of Ibn Sina's Life:

  • 980 - Born in Afshona, Bukhara, samanid persia(now present-day Uzbekistan)
  • 990- Memorised the entire Qur'an
  • 996- Started learning medicine and attending to the sick.Established as a physician by his own account and discovered new methods of treatment.
  • 997- Treated the Royal emir, Nuh II from a dangerous ilnesss
  • 998- Achieved full status as a qualified physician, also from his own account, and quoted with: "Medicine is no hard and thorny science, like mathematics and metaphysics, so I soon made great progress; I began to treat patients, using approved remedies." The youthful physician's fame spread quickly.
  • 1002- Lost his father and was forced to leave Bukhara and went to Urganj (present-day Konye-Urgench) in today’s Uzbekistan.
  • 1012- Out of necessity he moved to Gurgan (Jurjan or Gorgan) in Khurasan in search for a patron. He also started working on the Canon of Medicine which is his most famous work. He first met his disciple and scribe Juzjani.
  • 1013- He moved to Rai near the present-day Tehran to work as a physician.
  • 1015- He arrived in Hamadan where he settled down. He became vizier of Shams al-Dawla.
  • 1025- He arrived in Isfahan disguised as Sufi ascetics, along with his brother, a student and two slaves. They left Hamadan and arrived in Isfahan and were warmly welcomed by the city's ruler. He spent his last years in a relative peace, serving the city as the advisor of Isfahan's ruler and physician.
  • 1037- Died at the age of 57 in Hamadan, Iran
